Underwater inspection of ship hulls and hydraulic structures

Underwater Inspection Robot
Visionseeker VR01

Designed for high-precision diagnostics of the underwater sections of ship hulls and hydraulic structures, as well as for visual monitoring and mapping.

The robot combines exceptional maneuverability and stability in currents up to 3 knots, achieved through a dual-adhesion system — magnetic and thruster-based.

Equipped with:

Two HD cameras (including one optimized for turbid water)
LED lighting with a color temperature of 7800 K
Real-time video streaming system
Seaview FLS800D sonar for 3D object scanning

Questions & Answers:

  • What is the timeframe for cleaning one vessel?
    On average, processing a 180 m long vessel takes 12 hours — 5-6 times faster than traditional diver-based methods.
  • What fuel savings can be expected after cleaning?
    Depending on the degree of fouling, fuel savings may reach up to 30%. Our clients typically report savings of 15–30%.
  • Does the protective coating get damaged during cleaning?
    No. Our robots use a technology that removes only biological fouling without damaging the paint/coating.
  • What documents do you provide?
    After completion of the work we supply a full report with “before/after” photographs, an act of performed work, photo/video recording on electronic media, and all documents as per regulations.
